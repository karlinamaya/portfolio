What is Snout?
Snout was the capstone project of my Digital Media Technical Program certification that I obtained in 2021 through William T. McFatter Technical College. I was tasked to create the conceptual branding for a fictional company using my knowledge of visual design and UX concepts.
​
As an animal lover, I decided on the idea of Snout - a non-profit shelter looking to further its reach by offering a mobile app platform to adopt and provide care for animals.

Tools

  • Adobe Photoshop
  • Adobe Illustrator
  • Adobe After Effects

My Role

  • Branding
  • Visual design
  • UX research

Timeline

  • Overall: 14+ weeks
  • Research: 5+ weeks
  • Design: 9+ weeks

Personas

Understanding the target audience is essential for any successful design project. I began by developing three user personas in order to gain a stronger understanding of what a potential user base would look like for Snout. By delving into the needs, motivations, and pain points of these personas, I established a solid foundation for the project's design direction.

Logo Design

When designing the Snout logo, I had the concepts of simplicity, elegance, and cohesiveness in mind. I wanted to convey the unity and support that is represented through an animal shelter, prioritizing the care for animals as its main purpose. I felt that this design accurately met these intentions and carries the ability to flow well with additional design elements.

I created the logo early into the project timeline, as I wanted to establish the foundation of Snout's identity that I would be able to use as a framework to build the encompassing brand moving forward.

Wireframes

With the intent of turning ideas into tangible designs, I created the wireframes that evolved from the initial concept. Through a series of iterations, I refined the user interface and interaction flow to create an intuitive user experience. These photos showcase the intended user flow for the Snout app, as well as landing page prototypes for desktop, tablet, and smartphone devices.

Sample Web Logos

Along with the wireframes representing Snout's structure, I created potential web logos for the app. Snout's purpose is to provide a service that allows you to not only adopt animals, but shop for supplies and get in touch with local veterinarians as well. In order to account for the all-encompassing nature of Snout, I designed a variety of web logos that could be incorporated into the app's UI.

Landing Page

Once I had established a framework for Snout, I moved on to designing the landing page. My goal was to highlight the main mission of Snout and showcase a glimpse of the app's UI. Using Snout's logo as a framework for the surrounding brand, I focused on minimalism and cohesiveness in my design and content writing.

Animated Loading Screen

Even the smallest design details matter. To make the user experience smoother, I created an animated loading screen that could be implemented into the app for a pleasant transition during loading time. This screen was designed to match the brand's look and feel, making a strong first impression.

Once I established the basis of my vision for the platform, I created a few artifacts aimed at representing Snout's external image. 

Infographic

Infographics are a great way to convey information in a compact and direct manner, so I took this opportunity to share the key features of Snout. I wanted to express that Snout is not simply an adoption app - it contains a variety of functions that are necessary to new and current pet owners.

For the design, I utilized my previously made web logos to represent each key point. I stayed true to Snout's branding style, maintaining a clean and minimal look that is still fully expressive.

Web Banners

For the last project of my journey with Snout, I created a web banner design and adjusted it to fit multiple dimensions. The main challenge I had during the early stages of this design was determining how to best incorporate Snout's brand while also producing a design that would stand out enough in its own right to gain traction amidst the digital noise. After a series of brainstorming different ideas, I was able to come up with a design that I felt adhered to the Snout style and was eye-catching as well.
